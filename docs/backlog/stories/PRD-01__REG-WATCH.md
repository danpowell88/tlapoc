# Registration / PII / CPD expiry alerting

> **Epic:** [PRD-01 — Foundations & tenancy (auth, RBAC, audit, data model)](../epics/PRD-01.md)  ·  **Key:** `PRD-01/REG-WATCH`  ·  **Type:** Story  ·  **Stage:** M1  ·  **Priority:** P1  ·  **Estimate:** 3 pts  ·  **Area:** backend
>
> **Depends on:** `PRD-01/CREDENTIALS`

## Background

As a owner, I want to be alerted before a practitioner's registration, cosmetic insurance or CPD lapses, so that we never treat with an unregistered or uninsured practitioner.
Plainly: this watches every clinician's registration, insurance and training and warns before any of them lapses, so the clinic is never caught treating with an out-of-date practitioner. It builds straight on the credentials story. It feeds the owner's daily 'needs attention' list and the clinic's to-do queue, and on an actual lapse it automatically removes the practitioner from bookable slots — so it protects every booking and treatment that follows. Staff must be alerted before registration, insurance or CPD (continuing professional development) lapses so the clinic is never unknowingly non-compliant.

## How it works

A scheduled watch (on the Sprint-0 job scheduler) scans every credential daily and raises an alert for anything within N days of expiry — registration, cosmetic insurance, or CPD shortfall — so the clinic is never unknowingly non-compliant. The prototype shows the thresholds in practice: amber 'Renews in Nd' when reg or insurance is <=40 days out, and 'CPD behind X/Yh' when CPD done is under 60% of required.
On actual lapse the derived canInject (CREDENTIALS) flips to not-cleared automatically — the practitioner stops appearing as bookable (PRD-02) and S4 (Schedule 4 prescription-only medicine) administration by them is blocked (PRD-04/05). No human action is needed for the block; the alert exists to prompt renewal before that happens.
Alerts are role-targeted by concern: registration/insurance/CPD currency is a Lead/NP (nurse practitioner)/owner concern (the 'stockAlert'/'compliance'/'hr' concerns in the persona model), not every RN (registered nurse)'s. They feed the owner 'needs attention' digest (PRD-08) and project as expiry tasks into the Follow-ups queue (ADR-0023). Acknowledge/dismiss is audited so there's a record of who saw and actioned each warning.
AHPRA PIE (the AHPRA Practitioner Information Exchange register lookup) auto-verification re-checks status on a schedule where approved, with the manual-verify fallback always available; a status change pulled from PIE (e.g. suspended) is treated the same as a lapse for canInject.

## Acceptance Criteria

## Suggested data model

- **CredentialAlert** — id, tenant_id, credential_id, staff_id, type(expiring|lapsed|cpd_behind|status_change), due_date, severity(amber|red), acknowledged_by, acknowledged_at
  - _Generated by the scheduled watch (Sprint-0 job scheduler); projects to the owner digest + Follow-ups; acknowledge is audited._
