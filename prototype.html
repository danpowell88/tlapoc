        A prescriber will assess you and, if suitable, prescribe a Schedule 4 medicine administered by a registered nurse. Possible effects include bruising, swelling, headache, asymmetry or, rarely, drooping. Results are temporary (~3–4 months). Alternatives include doing nothing or non-injectable options. Costs are quoted before treatment. You can complain to us or to AHPRA at any time — a confidentiality agreement does not remove that right.
